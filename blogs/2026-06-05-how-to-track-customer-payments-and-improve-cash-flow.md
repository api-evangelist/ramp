Tracking customer payments means monitoring what each client owes, when payment is due, and what has been received, giving businesses clarity on cash position and early visibility into overdue invoices. Poor tracking creates cash flow disruptions through overlooked invoices, unrecorded receipts, and missed follow-ups that make predicting cash arrival nearly impossible. Effective payment tracking enables reliable cash flow forecasts, better spending decisions, and coordinated timing between receivables and expenditures.
